## r/japanlife Strategy

DO NOT post promotional content. Instead:

### 1. Monitor for Relevant Threads
Search for: "contractor", "renovation", "wallpaper", "flooring", "interior"

### 2. Answer Helpfully First
Example response to "How do I find a contractor?":

> Finding contractors as a foreigner is tough because most companies won't work with non-Japanese speakers. A few options:
>
> - Tokyo Handyman (expat-owned, good for small repairs)
> - Ai Construction (claims English service)
> - Some individual contractors work in English — harder to find but worth it for larger projects
>
> Main tips:
> 1. Always get written estimates (見積もり)
> 2. Ask about リフォーム (reform = renovation) not just 修理 (repair)
> 3. Get references from other expats if possible
>
> I work in interior finishing (wallpaper/flooring) and can answer questions about that specifically if helpful.

### 3. Build Reputation Before Promoting
- Answer 5-10 threads helpfully
- Mention services only when directly relevant
- Let reputation build organically
